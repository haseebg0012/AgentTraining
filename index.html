01
How the work actually flows

Intro to closing, step by step.

Every sale follows this exact sequence. Skipping a step is where deals fall apart, so run it in order every time.

1

Post in Facebook groups

You post about the relevant service in Facebook groups tied to the campaign you're running (country / product specific).

2

Watch comments AND message requests

Customers reach out two ways: they comment directly on your post, or they message you and it lands in your Message Requests folder, not your main inbox. Check your post's comments and your message requests constantly after posting.

Reply fast, a slow reply loses the customer
3

Pitch the right service

Understand what they need (country, number of users) and explain the matching package and price.

4

Deal agreed

Customer confirms they want the service. This is the point your commission gets locked in.

Commission starts here
5

Collect basic info, right in chat

Customer stays in the inbox, they don't fill anything themselves. You ask and note down First & Last Name, WhatsApp Number, Email Address, and DOB.

6

Tell the customer: department will message them

Let them know someone from the Setup & Verification Department will message them shortly. When that message lands, the customer replies to confirm and finalize the order.

7

You fill and submit the Google Form

The customer never touches the form. You fill it yourself with the customer's info, the package they're interested in, and your own name, then submit it.

8

Update the WhatsApp group

Right after submitting the form, post the customer's name in the WhatsApp group so the new sale gets logged on the sheet.

9

Track it, then follow up

Keep an eye on the group for confirmation that your customer's order is done. Follow up with the customer directly, and keep your own sale on your radar until it's marked complete.

Renewal commission depends on this
02
What you collect from the customer

You collect it, you submit it.

Once the deal is agreed, you get this directly in chat. The customer never fills anything themselves.

First & Last Name

Full legal name, used for the account and the form.

WhatsApp Number

Direct line for follow ups and department confirmation.

Email Address

Used for account setup on the platform they're buying.

Date of Birth

Required for verification on most platforms.

The customer never fills a form. You take this info in chat, then fill the Google Form yourself with the customer's details, the package they're interested in, and your own name, and submit it.

05
Situations you'll actually run into

Handle these the right way.

Every customer is a bit different. Here's how to think through the common situations.

Customer haggles

Prices are negotiable, so you have room to move. But never let a customer walk away just because they're pushing on price. Find a number that closes the deal.

Wants 1 user, not available

Countries like Ireland, Netherlands, Spain, France, Germany, Switzerland, Italy and Denmark only offer 3 to 5 user packages. Set that expectation early instead of promising a 1 user price you can't deliver.

Gulf / UAE client

Point them to Cisco Jabber, it's built specifically for UAE, Dubai and Gulf region clients.

Closes above listed price

Good outcome for both sides. The customer gets what they want, and your commission goes up with the final price.

Deal is done, then silence

Don't stop at confirmation. Keep the follow up going so you know the customer is set up properly, and so you're first in line when it's renewal time.

Sale logged, then what

After you post the sale in the WhatsApp group, keep checking it for the update on whether your customer's order is marked done. Follow up on the customer and on your own sale status at the same time, don't let either one slip.
